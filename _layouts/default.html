The Secrets of Primes

This is quite interesting — maybe even the most fascinating thing in human history. For years, we’ve developed the technology to send people beyond Earth and into space.

We’ve landed on the moon, created tools to explore the depths of the oceans, and ventured into the most mysterious parts of the sea. We’ve conquered the skies with fast jets, planes, and other aircraft.

We’ve discovered cures for diseases that once devastated humanity, and we developed effective vaccines for COVID-19 in less than a year. We’ve done all that—and so much more.

And yet, we remain haunted—or perhaps hunted — by one of the deepest secrets in mathematics: the mystery of the primes.

Their apparent randomness—though not truly random, as suggested by the Riemann Hypothesis and the known distribution of primes—still hides something profound.

It doesn’t reveal why.

And despite all this, we reduce their value to a seven-digit prize and the global appraisal of all mathematicians.

This is incredible.

— Daniel O. Esambu
